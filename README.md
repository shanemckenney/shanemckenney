# Shane McKenney

**Retired U.S. Army SFC · Cybersecurity Instructor · Developer · Veteran Mentor**

I spent 20 years in the Army — most of it in JAG, much of it in national security law, legal planning, and senior leadership — retired as a Sergeant First Class. Now I teach cybersecurity and IT at MyComputerCareer, where I help career changers, veterans, and people fighting imposter syndrome break into the field through practical labs, accountability, and the belief that discipline plus repetition beats raw talent every time.

Between classes, I build the tools I wish existed.

I also apparently build things at 2AM when someone says something that sounds vaguely like a challenge.

## The Teaching Philosophy

I don't teach people to pass tests. I teach people to think.

The certifications are the output. The real work is building the mental model — understanding *why* a false positive is different from a false negative, *why* CVSS without environmental context is just a number, *why* containment comes before eradication. When those click, the exam questions stop being trivia and start being obvious.

I focus especially on students coming from nontraditional backgrounds — veterans who don't know how to translate 20 years of leadership into a resume, career changers who've been told they started too late, people with ADHD or anxiety who learn differently and need a different approach. That's not charity work. Those students are often the best in the room.
